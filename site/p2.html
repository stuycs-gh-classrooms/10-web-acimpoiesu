New York Islanders

The New York Islanders, a professional ice hockey team based in Brooklyn and now Long Island, have faced their share of challenges and setbacks throughout their history. Established in 1972, the Islanders have had periods of both triumph and adversity on the ice. While the team experienced success in the early 1980s, winning four consecutive Stanley Cup championships from 1980 to 1983, they have struggled to replicate that level of success in recent years. Their most recent succesful season was in 2020 when they were 2 wins away from making the Stanley Cup Finals and they have not reached anywhere close since.

This is the Islanders' former home, Barclays center, which they left in 2020 and have played
in Long Island since.

Dominance in Early 80s

The New York Islanders of the early 1980s were a hockey powerhouse, establishing an unparalleled dynasty in the National Hockey League. They were coached by the esteemed Al Arbour, and boasted a formidable lineup featuring hall of fame players such as Bryan Trottier, Mike Bossy, and Denis Potvin. This combination of excellence by players and coaches allowed the Islanders to win 4 straight Stanley Cups which is tied for the 2nd highest streak.
