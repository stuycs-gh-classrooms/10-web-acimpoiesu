Hockey Overview

Hockey is played in a ovular rink with boards surrounding the perimeter and goal nets situated at each end. Each team typically consists of six players on the ice at a time: three forwards, two defensemen, and one goaltender. The game begins with a faceoff at center ice, where the puck (basically a flat ball) is dropped between two opposing players to start play. Players move across the ice using specialized ice skates, wielding hockey sticks to control and propel the puck. The primary objective is to maneuver the puck into the opposing team's goal net while defending their own net from scoring attempts. This requires precision stickhandling, passing, and shooting skills, as well as strategic positioning and teamwork to outmaneuver opponents and create scoring opportunities. A hockey game is split into 3, 20 minute, periods and like most sports whichever team has the most points by the end of that time wins.
