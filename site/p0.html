National Hockey League Overview

The National Hockey League is a professional ice hockey league consisting of 32 teams, divided into two conferences: the Eastern Conference and the Western Conference. Founded in 1917, the NHL is considered the premier professional ice hockey league in the world. It is actually the only major hockey league in the world and takes talent from all around the world (usually from cold places). Because of this it oversees the highest level of competition in professional hockey and hosts the annual Stanley Cup playoffs, which culminates in the awarding of the Stanley Cup trophy to the leagueâ€™s champion. Before this happens an 82 game regular season occurs to decide who makes the playoffs.

Stanley Cup Playoffs

The NHL playoffs are the culmination of the league's competitive season, where teams battle it out in a series of best-of-seven matchups to determine the ultimate champion. To understand this the NHL is randomly made into 3 divisions in each conference. Following the regular season, the best 3 teams from each division, along with 2 wild card entries per conference, qualify for the postseason. Seeding is determined by regular season standings, with higher-seeded teams enjoying home-ice advantage in the series. As teams progress through the rounds, the intensity ramps up, leading to the highly anticipated Stanley Cup Final, where the Eastern and Western Conference champions clash in a final showdown. Last year, the Las Vegas based team called the Las Vegas Golden Knights was able to become the Stanley Cup Champions by defeating the Florida Panthers 4-1.
